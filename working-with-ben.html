How I make teams better

I treat the systems around the work as part of the work. A few things I've built or driven that raised the floor for the whole team.

Design quality scoring
Design reviews were inconsistent — feedback varied by reviewer and standards weren't shared. Built a scoring rubric and review process used across BILL's design org, making quality expectations explicit and critiques more actionable.
Crit framework
Crits were often unstructured — designers didn't know what kind of feedback they were getting or what to do with it. Introduced a framing layer at the start of each crit (what's being asked, what stage the work is at) that made sessions faster and feedback more useful.
Presentation coaching
ICs were losing stakeholder confidence not because the work was weak, but because it was being communicated poorly. Worked 1:1 with designers on how to frame decisions, lead with insight, and anticipate the questions a cross-functional audience will actually ask.
Weekly design crit attendance
Show up every week — not to review my own work, but to help others improve theirs. That means direct feedback, connecting designers to prior work they can learn from, and warm introductions to subject matter experts who can unblock them.
Tools I've built for teammates

Same instinct as building my startups — I solve my own problem first, then figure out if others have the same one. Usually they do.

Career ladder tracker
Built a structured tracker that maps day-to-day work against the design career ladder — so ICs have a clear view of where they stand and what to prioritize to grow. Most senior designers don't build tools for their peers. This one got shared across the org.
Manager grading tool
Designed a self-assessment tool for design managers to evaluate their own performance against expectations — structured enough to surface real gaps, lightweight enough to actually use. Built it because the gap existed and someone needed to close it.
