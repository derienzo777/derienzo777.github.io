How I show up for the team

I attend weekly design review meetings — not just to review my own work, but to help other designers improve theirs. That means giving direct feedback, connecting people to previous work they can learn from, and making introductions to the subject matter experts who can unblock them.

I run workshops to get cross-functional teams genuinely aligned on a problem space — not just in the same room, but actually seeing the same thing. That takes facilitation, not just slides.

I want the team around me to be better. Not because it reflects well on me, but because better teammates make better products.

Tools I've built for other ICs

A performance management tracker so designers can log their wins in real time, not scramble to remember them at review season. A library of tips for using AI tools effectively in a design workflow. Templates and frameworks for presenting work more clearly to cross-functional stakeholders.

These aren't officially assigned projects. They're things I noticed were missing and built anyway — because the gap was real and the cost of not having them was paid by everyone.
